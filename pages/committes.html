UNGA

AGENDA - “ Hope or Hype? Jus Gentium: A Beacon of Hope or a Relic of the Past?”

A persistent question emerges when the oppressed's hope for justice flutters in their hearts. Can there be true justice when the winners set the terms for it? Like the convicts whose ambition has bound them, the champions of human rights frequently bear the burden of those who have prevailed. Self-interested, victorious people may ignore their own wrongdoings and concentrate only on the enemies they have defeated. This selectiveness produces a terrifying reality in which the very instruments designed to protect fundamental rights end up serving as tools of a distorted judicial system, permanently wounding the struggle for a better future. Is it possible for this distinguished assembly, committed to the principles of Jus Gentium, or The Law of Nations, to shatter these bonds and guarantee that justice extends beyond the winner's narrative. The world watches, for within these halls lies the potential to ignite a beacon of hope for a future illuminated by universal justice.

United Nations Security Council (UNSC)

AGENDA - “Confronting Global Tensions: The United Nations' Response to the South China Sea Standoff"

Geopolitically, the South China Sea is simmering, on the verge of exploding. Multi-nation claims to fragile islands serve as the focal point of a high-stakes maritime Monopoly game. The UN Security Council is not present to see this titanic struggle from the sidelines. The global commerce artery, freedom of navigation, is at risk of being clogged. Attempting to exert control unilaterally is like setting off a grenade in this delicate circumstance. We demand that all sides forgo the battleships and use diplomacy instead. By promoting communication and respect for international law, the UN Security Council can serve as a life preserver during this storm and guarantee a peaceful conclusion. Together, let's negotiate these dangerous waters before a minor altercation turns into a major conflict.

Lok Sabha

AGENDA - “Unveiling Constitutional Evolution: Delving into the Significance of the 43rd and 44th Amendments."

Amidst the shadows cast by the National Emergency, two constitutional milestones emerged, forever etching their significance in India's democratic journey. Born in the furnace of struggle, the 43rd and 44th Amendments serve as monuments to persistence and change. The former, in response to the challenges created by the Emergency, attempted to restore fundamental rights and counterbalance governmental power. Meanwhile, the latter, created with a historical perspective, strengthened democratic values and protected individual liberty. As we convene to reflect on these chapters in our constitutional history, we commemorate the spirit of democracy and the unwavering pursuit of justice.

UNODC

AGENDA - “Beyond Black and White: Exploring the Gray Areas of Drug Policy."

Decades of drug warfare have left a human devastation in their wake. The UNODC suggests switching to controlled legalization in exchange for a ceasefire. Imagine a well-lit pharmacy with age verification and quality control taking the place of a criminal bazaar. Legalization destroys criminal networks, raises tax income to combat addiction, and substitutes safe pharmaceuticals for tainted ones.
Though some people worry about an increase in use, legalization isn't a free-for-all. Compare it to cigarettes in that it is controlled, subject to taxes, and surrounded by anti-smoking campaigns. Not with closed fists, but with open discourse, is what the UNODC calls for. One educated discussion at a time, let's discard the outdated tactics of the past and construct a healthy future, piece by piece, through evidence-based policies.

The agenda of tackling police brutality is akin to unraveling a gripping mystery, delving deep into the complexities of human behavior, systemic flaws, and the delicate balance between security and civil liberties. In this mission, the UNHRC shines as a symbol of hope, sparking talks, building understanding, and driving real change toward a world where uniforms mean fairness and safety for everyone, not just power.
